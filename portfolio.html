Large-Scale Solar Panel & Battery Installation Program

Ongoing
Mar 2024 - Present Octopus Energy 8 Engineers, 12 Installers, 5 Customer Service Reps 250+ Installations

Project Overview

When I took over this program in March 2024, the team was struggling with scheduling conflicts, customer communication gaps, and weather-related delays that were impacting customer satisfaction. Through systematic process improvements and proactive risk management, I transformed this into a high-performing program delivering consistent results across 250+ installations.

Challenges

  • High volume of installations (15+ per month) creating scheduling conflicts
  • Complex coordination between multiple stakeholders (engineers, installers, customers, contractors)
  • Regulatory compliance requirements varying by region
  • Weather-dependent installation delays affecting project timelines
  • Customer communication gaps leading to dissatisfaction
  • Inventory management for multiple product types (Enphase, Tesla, Givenergy, Fox)

Solution

  • Implemented proactive risk management framework identifying potential delays 2 weeks in advance
  • Optimized Kraken CRM processes to streamline customer communication and workflow automation
  • Developed comprehensive project scheduling system with buffer time for weather delays
  • Created standardized installation checklists ensuring 100% compliance across all regions
  • Established clear escalation pathways for complex installations requiring additional resources
  • Implemented real-time project tracking dashboard for stakeholders
  • Introduced customer engagement touchpoints at key project milestones

Installation Process Overview

The solar and battery installation journey follows a structured 4-step process ensuring regulatory compliance, customer readiness, and seamless execution:

Step 1: Getting Ready for Installation (28 working days)
  • DNO Applications: Submit G98/G99/G100 applications to Distribution Network Operators (DNOs) to notify the local grid of energy export. G99 applications are submitted prior to installation, while G98 is post-installation commissioning. G100 applications handle export limitations when DNOs propose restrictions.
  • Structural Calculations: Complete structural calculations for trussed roofs using OpenSolar to ensure roof load capacity. For cut roofs, calculations are performed on-site during installation day 1.
  • Earth Rod Installation: Coordinate earthing rod installations for Tesla Powerwall customers to enable Whole-home Backup functionality and ensure electrical compliance.
  • Smart Meter Verification: Verify customer has smart meter installed (S1 or S2ADE type) to access smart export tariffs. Book smart meter upgrades for Octopus Energy customers if required.
  • HIES Registration: Verify HIES (Home Insulation & Energy Systems) warranty registration completed by Sales team - a mandatory compliance requirement.
  • Install Readiness Activities: Support customers with clearing space for battery locations, cable runs, and any property-specific requirements.
  • Scaffolding Preparation: Initiate scaffolding form preparation during Step 1 to expedite Step 2 scheduling.
  • Isolator Switch Checks: Verify isolator switch requirements for Northern Powergrid customers or slate roof installations.
Step 2: Scheduling and Install Booked (42 days average wait)
  • Pre-Booking Verification: Confirm DNO approval status (must be approved or submitted 3+ months before install date), structural calculations completed for trussed roofs, and earth rod completed for Tesla customers.
  • Scaffolding Booking: Book third-party scaffolding ensuring erection 3+ days before installation date. Coordinate with regional suppliers and verify customer access requirements.
  • Material Picklist Verification: Verify design team has uploaded correct material picklist to Kraken Field at least 3-4 weeks before installation date.
  • Export Tariff Discussion: Discuss smart export tariff options (Outgoing Octopus, Octopus Flux, Intelligent Octopus Flux, Outgoing Agile) with customers, explaining 4-6 week onboarding timeframe.
  • Customer Communication: Maintain regular contact every 7-10 days, setting expectations on installation timeline and process.
  • Northern Powergrid Isolator: Book isolator switch installation for Northern Powergrid customers requiring specific qualifications.
Step 3: Supporting Through Installation (1-3 working days)
  • Scaffolding Verification: Contact scaffolding supplier day before erection to confirm readiness. Call customer and supplier on erection day to verify successful completion.
  • Installation Day Coordination: Contact customer on installation morning to outline process and answer questions. Maintain regular communication throughout installation days.
  • Field Team Liaison: Coordinate with Area Managers (AMs) for progress updates, issue resolution, and customer communication.
  • Issue Escalation: Promptly flag and escalate any delays, unexpected findings, or technical issues to AMs or project team.
  • Cut Roof Structural Calculations: Ensure install team completes structural calculations for cut roofs on day 1 and uploads to Kraken Field for Bespoke/BMG review.
Step 4: Post-Installation (14 days)
  • Customer Care Call: Conduct courtesy call within 24 hours to verify installation completion and customer satisfaction.
  • G98/G99 Commissioning: Complete G98 commissioning documentation for DNO notification post-installation, or verify G99 approval status.
  • MCS Certification: Generate and complete MCS (Microgeneration Certification Scheme) certificates ensuring compliance with UK renewable energy standards.
  • HIES Completion: Mark installation as complete on HIES Installer Hub, linking warranty to completed installation.
  • NAPIT Registration: Register installation with NAPIT (National Association of Professional Inspectors and Testers) for building regulations compliance.
  • NIC Certificate: Complete NIC (NICEIC) certification for battery installations ensuring electrical safety standards.
  • Export Tariff Setup: Initiate export tariff onboarding process (4-6 weeks) for customers choosing smart export options.
  • Scaffolding Dismantle: Arrange scaffolding removal post-installation completion.
  • Handover Pack: Generate and send comprehensive handover pack including MCS certificates, warranty information, system manuals, and PV array test documents (Tesla customers).
  • Payment Processing: Request final payment/invoice and verify SAT (Site Acceptance Test) notes uploaded for finance customers.
  • System Activation: Mark customer as "Live" in system, completing installation journey.

Impact & Results

  • 98% on-time delivery rate (vs. 85% industry average) - 13 percentage points above benchmark
  • 30% reduction in project delays (from baseline of 22% delayed projects to 15% delayed) through proactive risk identification
  • 40% increase in customer engagement efficiency (reduced average response time from 48 hours to 19 hours) via Kraken CRM optimization
  • 15% boost in customer retention through improved communication
  • 20% improvement in project completion rates via better resource allocation
  • 25% reduction in project turnaround times (from 12 weeks to 9 weeks average) - 3 weeks faster delivery
  • 18% reduction in compliance errors through standardized processes
  • 250+ successful installations across multiple product lines
  • Managed installations of 450W high-performance PV panels with 25-year warranties
  • Coordinated Tesla Powerwall 3 battery installations with expansion packs

EV Charger Infrastructure Installation Initiative

Ongoing
Jun 2024 - Present Octopus Energy 5 Installation Specialists, 3 Electricians, 2 Customer Advisors 50+ Installations

Project Overview

Coordinating the installation of electric vehicle charging infrastructure for residential customers, aligning engineers, electricians and suppliers while ensuring seamless integration with existing electrical systems and Octopus Energy smart tariffs.

Challenges

  • Complex electrical assessments required for each property
  • Integration with existing solar systems and battery storage
  • Coordination with DNO (Distribution Network Operator) for grid connections
  • Customer education needed on smart tariff benefits (Intelligent Octopus Flux)
  • Managing multiple product specifications (Octopus charge, Ohmi Pro, Hypervolt)
  • Ensuring compatibility with various EV models
  • Tight installation windows due to customer vehicle usage patterns

Solution

  • Developed pre-installation assessment framework reducing on-site issues by 35%
  • Created integration protocols for solar-battery-EV charger systems
  • Established direct communication channels with DNO for faster approvals
  • Implemented customer education program on smart tariffs and usage optimization
  • Built product compatibility matrix ensuring correct charger selection per customer
  • Introduced flexible scheduling system accommodating customer preferences
  • Automated post-installation setup processes in Kraken CRM

Installation Process Overview

The EV charger installation journey follows a streamlined 4-step process ensuring electrical safety, regulatory compliance, and optimal smart tariff integration:

Step 1: Getting Ready for Installation (1-21 days)
  • Smart Meter Verification: Verify customer has smart meter (S1 or S2ADE type) to access smart EV charging tariffs. Book smart meter upgrades for Octopus Energy customers if required.
  • Customer Survey Review: Review post-sale survey photos assessing meter cupboard, parking space, cable routes, and electrical setup. Verify off-street parking availability and continuous wall for cable routing.
  • Max Demand Calculation: Calculate maximum electrical demand including EV charger load. Determine if existing fuse cut-out rating can handle additional load.
  • DNO ENA Application: Submit ENA (Energy Networks Association) application to DNO when max demand exceeds existing fuse rating, looped supply detected, or old-style fuse identified. Typical response time 1-2 weeks.
  • Grant Applications: Register customers through OZEV (Office for Zero Emissions Vehicles) portal for tenant grants, or WCS (Workplace Charging Scheme) portal for workplace installations.
  • Install Readiness Activities: Support customers with groundworks (trenching for detached charger locations), post installation for cable routing, or third-party electrician requirements. Coordinate via Checkatrade referrals when needed.
  • Permissions: Verify permissions for rented properties, listed buildings, or nature reserve locations before proceeding with installation.
  • Three-Phase Supply Assessment: Identify if customer has 3-phase supply and whether all phases are in use, determining if specialized engineer required.
Step 2: Scheduling and Install Booked (24+ hours)
  • Customer Scheduling: Schedule installation via autoscheduler on Kraken Field, or provide self-serve booking link (expires after 48 hours). Contact capacity team if no dates available.
  • Octopus Charge Compatibility: Verify Octopus Energy supply and working smart meter for Octopus Charge customers (Intelligent Octopus Go tariff requirement).
  • Engineer Notes: Add comprehensive installation notes to Kraken Field jobcard including complaint history, site survey notes, agreed cable routes, and ticket owner contact details.
  • Customer Expectations: Inform customers of power outage duration (up to 1 hour) and installation timeline: Standard (4 hours), Standard Plus (5 hours), Enhanced (6 hours).
  • Pre-Install Verification: Confirm DNO works completed, groundworks finished, and all install readiness activities resolved before installation date.
  • Regular Check-ins: Maintain contact every 10 days minimum, requesting images of completed pre-install works.
Step 3: Supporting Through Installation (4-6 hours typical)
  • In-Day Support: Be available for customer contact during installation, addressing questions and coordinating with field teams.
  • Failed Install Handling: For installations in "Install In Progress" status 24+ hours, check Kraken Field job status, understand issues, and call customer with next steps.
  • Field Coordination: Liaise with engineers and Area Managers to resolve on-site issues, technical challenges, or customer concerns.
  • Issue Escalation: Escalate to field management if job not closed within 24 hours, ensuring prompt resolution.
Step 4: Post-Installation (7-14 days for grant customers)
  • Grant Completion: Complete OZEV or WCS grant applications for eligible customers, submitting installation evidence and customer details.
  • Smart Tariff Onboarding: Guide customers through Intelligent Octopus Go onboarding process, providing charger-specific instructions (Ohme, Zappi, Hypervolt) and tariff information.
  • Courtesy Call: Conduct post-installation check-in call to verify customer satisfaction and address any questions.
  • System Verification: Confirm charger functionality, app connectivity, and smart tariff integration.

Impact & Results

  • 50+ successful installations across multiple charger brands
  • 90% positive customer feedback on installation experience
  • Zero installation-related electrical issues through thorough pre-assessment
  • Average 3-day installation timeline from assessment to completion
  • 100% integration success with existing solar/battery systems
  • 80% customer adoption rate of Intelligent Octopus Flux smart tariff
  • Reduced customer support queries by 25% through improved education
  • Streamlined DNO approval process reducing delays by 40%

Residential Heat Pump Installation Program

Ongoing
Aug 2024 - Present Octopus Energy 6 HVAC Engineers, 4 Plumbers, 3 Energy Advisors 20+ Installations

Project Overview

Managing residential heat pump installation projects, coordinating complex heating system replacements while ensuring minimal disruption to customers and maximizing energy efficiency benefits.

Challenges

  • Complex property assessments for heat pump suitability
  • Removal of existing gas/oil heating systems
  • Coordination with multiple trades (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
  • Managing customer expectations during installation (can take 3-5 days)
  • Ensuring proper insulation and heat loss calculations
  • Multiple product types requiring different installation approaches (Cosy 6, Cosy 9, Cosy 12, Diken)
  • Weather dependency for outdoor unit installation
  • Post-installation commissioning and system optimization

Solution

  • Developed comprehensive property assessment framework including heat loss calculations
  • Created detailed installation timeline with customer communication at each stage
  • Established multi-trade coordination protocols ensuring seamless handoffs
  • Implemented customer accommodation support during installation period
  • Built product selection guide based on property size and heating requirements
  • Introduced weather contingency planning with flexible scheduling
  • Established post-installation optimization process with follow-up support
  • Integrated heat pump installations with solar/battery systems where applicable

Installation Process Overview

The heat pump installation journey follows a comprehensive 4-step process ensuring regulatory compliance, property readiness, and seamless multi-trade coordination:

Step 1: Getting Ready for Installation (75 days with planning permission, 30 days without)
  • Smart Meter Verification: Verify customer has smart meter (S1 or S2ADE type) to access smart heating tariffs. Book smart meter upgrades for Octopus Energy customers if required.
  • DNO ENA Application: Submit ENA application to Distribution Network Operator notifying intention to install heat pump. DNO assesses if electrical supply can handle additional demand. Typical response 1-2 weeks, with DNO often performing required upgrades at no charge.
  • HIES Registration Verification: Verify HIES warranty registration completed by Sales team - mandatory compliance requirement preventing delays at installation completion.
  • Major Install Readiness Activities: Coordinate planning permission applications (75-day timeline), asbestos testing and removal via Crucial Environmental, structural surveys for property strengthening, and repipe works for unsuitable pipework. Support customers through planning team liaisons and mitigation options.
  • Install Readiness Activities: Support customers with clearing space for hot water cylinders, heat pump outdoor unit locations, trenching for ground source systems, and groundworks coordination.
  • Customer Communication: Maintain contact every 7 days minimum, providing updates on readiness activities and DNO application progress.
Step 2: Scheduling and Install Booked (3 days to schedule, ~42 days waiting for install date)
  • Pre-Booking Verification: Confirm all major install readiness activities completed at least 3 weeks before installation date. Verify DNO approval received and uploaded to HubSpot/Arrows.
  • BUS Grant Application: Apply for Boiler Upgrade Scheme (BUS) voucher through government portal, obtaining voucher number for customer grant eligibility.
  • EPC Verification: Check customer's Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) for recommendations and obtain EPC reference number for BUS application.
  • Installation Booking: Book installation on Kraken Field using job time calculator, confirming date with customer before finalizing booking.
  • Job Notes & Logistics: Add comprehensive handover notes to Kraken Field booking including customer preferences, property specifics, bespoke parts, radiator replacement count, and heat pump type (Daikin or Cosy).
  • Gas Meterworks & Capping: Coordinate gas capping at boiler (other gas appliances present), gas capping at meter (no other gas appliances), or gas meter removal (Octopus Energy customers, removes standing charge).
  • Scaffolding Booking: Book third-party scaffolding when required for pipe runs or cable installations at height, coordinating with regional suppliers.
  • Pre-Delivery Coordination: Verify warehouse awareness of bespoke parts (Eddi solar diverters, solar thermal cylinders) and confirm material schedules.
  • Field Team Communication: Contact assigned lead installers via Slack channels, providing installation details, customer requirements, and any special considerations.
Step 3: Supporting Through Installation (5 days maximum)
  • Day 1 Customer Call: Contact customer on first day of installation to check progress, address questions, and provide updates.
  • Waste Removal Booking: Book Clearabee waste collection for day after scheduled completion, ensuring proper disposal of old heating system components.
  • Field Team Updates: Update field teams via Slack and customer notes in HubSpot as installation progresses, coordinating any required adjustments.
  • In-Week Issue Resolution: Address any installation issues, delays, or customer concerns promptly, liaising with Area Managers and field crews.
  • Completion Verification: Check Kraken Field for installation completion status and contact customer on final day to confirm system commissioning.
Step 4: Post-Installation (14 days)
  • Courtesy Call: Conduct post-installation customer care call to verify satisfaction and address any questions.
  • BUS Grant Redemption: Redeem BUS voucher through government portal, submitting installation evidence and completing grant claim process.
  • MCS Certificate: Complete MCS (Microgeneration Certification Scheme) certificate registration, ensuring compliance with UK renewable heating standards.
  • HIES Completion: Mark installation as complete on HIES Installer Hub, finalizing warranty registration.
  • Warranty Registration: Register Daikin installations through Daikin Standby Me portal, activating warranty coverage. Register with NAPIT for building regulations compliance.
  • EIC Audit Verification: Verify EIC (Electrical Installation Certificate) audits completed via shared spreadsheet before sending handover packs.
  • Service Plan Setup: Add service plan to customer's Kraken account if applicable, ensuring ongoing maintenance coverage.
  • Scaffolding Dismantle: Arrange scaffolding removal post-installation if required.
  • Handover Pack: Download and send comprehensive handover pack via Arrows or WeTransfer including MCS certificates, warranty information, system manuals, and operating instructions.
  • Payment Processing: Send final invoice and chase payment if not received within 5 days of handover pack delivery.
  • Final Close-Off: Send final close-off email confirming installation completion and providing ongoing support contact information.

Impact & Results

  • 20+ successful heat pump installations across different property types
  • 100% customer satisfaction on installation quality and process
  • Average 4-day installation timeline meeting project schedules
  • Zero post-installation system failures through thorough commissioning
  • Average 50-70% reduction in heating costs for customers
  • 90% customer retention with heat pump systems
  • Reduced installation-related complaints by 60% through improved communication
  • Successfully coordinated 5 integrated solar-battery-heat pump systems
