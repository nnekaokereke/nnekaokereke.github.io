Project Overview

When I took over this program in March 2024, the team was struggling with scheduling conflicts, customer communication gaps, and weather-related delays that were impacting customer satisfaction. Through systematic process improvements and proactive risk management, I transformed this into a high-performing program delivering consistent results across 250+ installations.

Challenges

  • High volume of installations (15+ per month) creating scheduling conflicts
  • Complex coordination between multiple stakeholders (engineers, installers, customers, contractors)
  • Regulatory compliance requirements varying by region
  • Weather-dependent installation delays affecting project timelines
  • Customer communication gaps leading to dissatisfaction
  • Inventory management for multiple product types (Enphase, Tesla, Givenergy, Fox)

Solution

  • Implemented proactive risk management framework identifying potential delays 2 weeks in advance
  • Optimized HubSpot CRM processes to streamline customer communication and workflow automation
  • Created standardized installation checklists ensuring 100% compliance across all regions
  • Established clear escalation pathways for complex installations requiring additional resources
  • Implemented real-time project tracking dashboard for stakeholders
  • Introduced customer engagement touchpoints at key project milestones

Installation Process Overview

I manage the solar and battery programme through a structured four-phase approach, maintaining alignment between engineers, customers and grid operators from initial project handover through to export tariff activation. This disciplined methodology ensures consistent delivery standards while adapting to each installation's unique requirements.

Step 1 – Getting Ready for Installation (≈ four weeks)

As soon as sales hand me a customer I complete the compliance triage: confirming the HIES warranty registration, pulling the final sales agreement into HubSpot and submitting the relevant G98, G99 or G100 application to the Distribution Network Operator. While the DNO reviews the design I walk the customer through structural requirements, arrange OpenSolar calculations for trussed roofs and book onsite calculations for cut roofs. Tesla Powerwall customers receive an earthing rod briefing, Octopus Energy customers get a smart meter upgrade if they still have a legacy meter, and I capture photos and access notes so our scaffolding partners can quote without a site visit. Weekly check-ins and CRM tasking keep every readiness action visible to the customer and the wider LCT team.

Step 2 – Scheduling and Locking In Logistics

Once the DNO approval lands (or is safely pending three months ahead of install) I can book the job in Kraken Field. I double-check structural calculations, earth rod status and battery locations, then raise scaffolding requests with the appropriate regional supplier so the build is up at least three days before the crew arrives. I liaise with design to ensure the material picklist is uploaded in good time, brief the warehouse on any bespoke parts, and update installation notes so the field lead knows exactly what to expect. Customers also receive an export-tariff teach-in covering Outgoing Octopus, Flux and Intelligent Flux so they understand the four-to-six-week onboarding window.

Step 3 – Supporting the Installation

The week of delivery starts with a call to the scaffolding partner and the customer to confirm safe access. During the install I remain the single point of contact: arranging waste collections where required, relaying daily progress from the installers, and escalating through Area Managers if weather or design snags threaten the schedule. For cut roofs I chase the structural report the same day it is uploaded so the customer's handover pack is complete.

Step 4 – Post-Installation Close-Out

Within 24 hours of commissioning I carry out a courtesy call, confirm the G98/G99 paperwork has been submitted to the DNO and trigger the MCS certificate. I finalise HIES, NAPIT and NICEIC registrations, assemble the handover pack (including PV array tests and warranty information) and send this off to the customer. Finally, I raise the invoice, chase payment if needed, arrange scaffolding dismantling, and monitor the export tariff onboarding until the customer is officially live.

EV Charger Infrastructure Installation Initiative

Ongoing
Jun 2024 - Present Octopus Energy 5 Installation Specialists, 3 Electricians, 2 Customer Advisors 50+ Installations

Project Overview

Coordinating the installation of electric vehicle charging infrastructure for residential customers, aligning engineers, electricians and suppliers while ensuring seamless integration with existing electrical systems and Octopus Energy smart tariffs.

Challenges

  • Complex electrical assessments required for each property
  • Integration with existing solar systems and battery storage
  • Coordination with DNO (Distribution Network Operator) for grid connections
  • Customer education needed on smart tariff benefits (Intelligent Octopus Flux)
  • Ensuring compatibility with various EV models
  • Tight installation windows due to customer vehicle usage patterns

Solution

  • Introduced a readiness triage that captures ENA status, smart meter upgrades and grant evidence to prevent day-of-installation blockers
  • Produced installation playbooks combining site photos, load calculations and cable routing notes so electricians arrive prepared
  • Sequenced DNO liaison, customer groundworks and engineer scheduling through HubSpot timelines and Kraken Field checkpoints
  • Set structured customer touchpoints (readiness call, day-before confirmation, installation updates, post-install wrap-up) to maintain transparency
  • Coordinated with solar and battery teams to validate load management and integration before the charger goes live
  • Monitored live jobs via Kraken Field to trigger rapid escalations, resource swaps or contingency plans when progress slipped
  • Delivered tailored onboarding packs and smart tariff coaching to accelerate adoption immediately after commissioning

Installation Process Overview

Residential EV charging sounds simple: mount a unit and plug in, but every driveway has a different grid profile, grant eligibility and customer confidence level. I therefore run the initiative as a four-beat journey that blends technical due diligence with concierge-style communication.

Step 1 – Getting Ready for Installation

I start by analysing the customer’s post-sale survey: meter cupboard photos, proposed cable route, parking arrangements and any existing solar or battery integrations. That informs my maximum-demand calculation and whether an ENA notification or approval is required before work begins. Where demand exceeds the current cut-out or the property shares a supply, I submit applications to the DNO and set expectations on typical response times. In parallel I verify the presence of a smart meter (booking an upgrade if they are with Octopus and still on a legacy meter), guide tenants through OZEV grant eligibility, and arrange permissions for rentals, listed buildings or conservation areas. Any groundworks or third-party electrical works are scoped, costed and tracked in HubSpot so nothing surprises the customer later on.

Step 2 – Scheduling and Engineer Briefing

Once the property is technically ready, I either release a self-serve booking link or schedule the installation manually through Kraken Field. Every booking is accompanied by detailed engineer notes that cover grant status, cable routing decisions, meter location constraints, complaint history and my direct contact details. I also reconfirm that any prerequisite DNO works or customer groundworks are complete, share the Standard/Standard Plus/Enhanced duration guidance, and set reminders to chase customers for photo evidence of completed readiness tasks.

Step 3 – Supporting the Installation Day

During the install window I monitor the job card in Kraken Field and stay on-call for both the lead electrician and the homeowner. If anything stalls, perhaps a fuse upgrade has not been completed or an unexpected cable route is required, I coordinate with Area Managers or the compliance team to unblock it and keep the customer informed. Should an installation slip into “in progress” for more than a day, I call the customer first, document the root cause and set an immediate recovery plan.

Step 4 – Post-Install Enablement

After commissioning I file the OZEV or WCS grant evidence, trigger the Intelligent Octopus Go onboarding journey and send charger-specific setup resources (Ohme, Zappi or Hypervolt). A follow-up call confirms the charger is performing, the app is connected and the customer understands smart tariff behaviours. Only when the tariff switch is confirmed and any snag list is closed do I mark the job complete.

Impact & Results

  • 50+ successful installations across multiple charger brands
  • 90% positive customer feedback on installation experience
  • Zero installation-related electrical issues through thorough pre-assessment
  • Average 3-day installation timeline from assessment to completion
  • 100% integration success with existing solar/battery systems
  • 80% customer adoption rate of Intelligent Octopus Flux smart tariff
  • Reduced customer support queries by 25% through improved education

Residential Heat Pump Installation Program

Ongoing
Aug 2024 - Present Octopus Energy 6 HVAC Engineers, 4 Plumbers, 3 Energy Advisors 20+ Installations

Project Overview

Managing residential heat pump installation projects, coordinating complex heating system replacements while ensuring minimal disruption to customers and maximizing energy efficiency benefits.

Challenges

  • Complex property assessments for heat pump suitability
  • Removal of existing gas/oil heating systems
  • Coordination with multiple trades (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
  • Managing customer expectations during installation (can take 3-5 days)
  • Ensuring proper insulation and heat loss calculations
  • Multiple product types requiring different installation approaches (Cosy 6, Cosy 9, Cosy 12, Diken)
  • Weather dependency for outdoor unit installation
  • Post-installation commissioning and system optimization

Solution

  • Developed comprehensive property assessment framework including heat loss calculations
  • Created detailed installation timeline with customer communication at each stage
  • Established multi-trade coordination protocols ensuring seamless handoffs
  • Implemented customer accommodation support during installation period
  • Built product selection guide based on property size and heating requirements
  • Introduced weather contingency planning with flexible scheduling
  • Established post-installation optimization process with follow-up support
  • Integrated heat pump installations with solar/battery systems where applicable

Installation Process Overview

Heat pumps ask far more of a home than a like-for-like boiler swap, so I steward every project through a detailed readiness, delivery and aftercare cycle. The blueprint below translates the OES Low Carbon Tech handbook into a customer-facing journey.

Step 1 – Preparing the Property (30–75 days depending on planning)

The moment a ticket lands I run compliance checks—confirming the HIES policy, logging the sale in HubSpot and submitting an ENA application so the DNO can confirm the supply can take the additional electrical load. I book an introductory call within 24 hours to map the property, discuss the proposed indoor and outdoor locations and agree the cadence of updates. From there I orchestrate the major readiness activities: engaging the planning team for conservation or listed properties, ordering asbestos sampling and removal through Crucial Environmental, arranging structural surveys where loft cylinders or new plant rooms are required, and scoping any repipe or radiator upgrades. Customers receive bespoke checklists, and I maintain weekly updates so they never feel adrift during the long-lead tasks.

Step 2 – Scheduling and Logistics

When every blocker is cleared, I secure the installation slot in Kraken Field, making sure it respects the install length derived from our heat-loss calculations. I raise the Boiler Upgrade Scheme voucher, capture the EPC reference, and decide whether the customer needs gas capping, meter removal or a smart meter appointment. Scaffolding and lifting equipment are booked for homes that require high-level pipework, and I brief the warehouse on any bespoke components (for example buffer tanks or solar diverters). The Kraken Field job notes capture radiator counts, product families (Cosy or Daikin), access complexities and my direct contact details so the lead engineer, plumbers and electricians have a shared source of truth.

Step 3 – Coordinating the Install Week

Installation weeks span multiple trades, so I check in on day one to confirm the crew has arrived, the old system is draining down safely and the schedule is holding. I coordinate Clearabee waste collections, monitor gas metering appointments, and resolve snags—be that a weather delay for the external unit or a late delivery of bespoke materials—by looping in Area Managers or logistics. Daily notes go back into HubSpot and Slack to keep customer, field and office teams perfectly aligned.

Step 4 – Commissioning, Grants and Handover

Within 24 hours of commissioning I place a courtesy call, redeem the BUS voucher, and ensure the MCS certificate, HIES completion and NAPIT notification are filed. I register Daikin systems on Standby Me, assemble the digital handover pack (including heat-loss reports, warranties and user guides) and request scaffold strike. Payment, snag tracking and the first-service reminder are all scheduled before I mark the customer live and transition them to aftercare.

Impact & Results

  • 20+ successful heat pump installations across different property types
  • 100% customer satisfaction on installation quality and process
  • Average 4-day installation timeline meeting project schedules
  • Zero post-installation system failures through thorough commissioning
  • Average 50-70% reduction in heating costs for customers
  • 90% customer retention with heat pump systems
  • Reduced installation-related complaints by 60% through improved communication
  • Successfully coordinated 5 integrated solar-battery-heat pump systems
